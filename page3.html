    • Hello again! if you're here, it is because you are curious about me and my life. Good thing I am an open book, and can share some things about myself with you today.

    I am a Brazilian who considers Japan as their home. Growing up in that beautiful country helped me become the woman that I am today. From learning traditional cultures and ancient manners to living in big cities and seeing how time has changed the country, I have experienced everything that I could possibly imagine in Japan.

    I have also lived in China for two years, where I experienced culture shock for the first time. Not only I was in a country in which I could somehow read some of the text (for being the same as Japanese) but did not speak the language itself, I also had to learn a third language to survive in an international school. My mother refused to homeschool me, knowing that I was someone that would make studying my last priority at the time.

    And now I am here in the United States studying journalism and trying to figure out my future.

    I love researching about cinema and TV, and that's when my writing skills come in handy. A fun fact about me is that I started a research paper on feminism and the portray of female characters in Hollywood JUST to apply to SAIC. And I easily wrote twelve pages on this topic. And I continue researching and analysing shows just for this purpose.
